Confidence menu commands

The Confidence menu offers commands to adjust the confidence intervals displayed on the density function plot. You can adjust the size of the interval by changing the confidence level. A higher confidence level results in larger intervals:

68.3% Confidence intervals are 1.0 standard deviations for simple intervals and ChiSq=2.3 for joint intervals.
90% Confidence intervals are 1.6 standard deviations for simple intervals and ChiSq=4.6 for joint intervals.
99.5% Confidence intervals are 2 standard deviations for simple intervals and ChiSq=6.2 for joint intervals.
99% Confidence intervals are 2.6 standard deviations for simple intervals and ChiSq=9.2 for joint intervals.
99.73% Confidence intervals are 3 standard deviations for simple intervals and ChiSq=11.8 for joint intervals.
99.99% Confidence intervals are 3.9 standard deviations for simple intervals and ChiSq=18.4 for joint intervals.
The user can select between simple confidence ellipses or joint confidence ellipses. Joint ellipses will better simulate the results of a Monte Carlo simulation.
Simple Confidence interval ellipses are calculated for each variable, independently. Error ellipses (intensity vs. rate or intensity vs. time are always alligned on the cartesian grid.
Joint Confidence interval ellipses are calculated jointly for each term in the density function. This will generally results in ellipses that are not alligned on the cartesian grid, but are tilted. Typically, terms that are close to one another are tilted toward each other. The ellipses can appear curved due to the non-linear x-axis.
